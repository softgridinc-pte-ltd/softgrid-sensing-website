6. Frequency Domain Insights

Frequency analysis reveals which mechanical components may be degrading. Different frequency bands correspond to different lift subsystems:

  • 0–2 Hz: Normal ride motion (acceleration/deceleration)
  • 2–10 Hz: Guide rail irregularities, roller wear
  • 10–30 Hz: Motor, gearbox, drive system
  • 30–50 Hz: Brake, sheave, high-frequency mechanical

7. Honest Assessment

What Vibration Tells Us That the Database Doesn't

  • Direct physical measurement: Vibration is what the lift actually experiences, not what the controller interprets. It can detect mechanical degradation before error codes trigger.
  • Frequency decomposition: Different frequency bands map to specific components (guide rails, motor, brakes), enabling targeted maintenance.
  • Continuous monitoring: Every trip produces a vibration signature, vs sparse error/fault events in the database.

What Vibration CANNOT Tell Us

  • Electrical faults: Wiring, controller, or sensor issues may not manifest in vibration.
  • Intermittent issues: A fault that occurs once every 1000 trips may not be captured in a 10-trip sample.
  • Causation: Elevated vibration before a fault is correlation, not proof of causation. Both may share a common upstream cause.

Limitations of This Analysis

  • Sample size: 12 lifts, 914 trips. Statistical power is limited for detecting small effects.
  • Period selection: "Normal" vs "pre-fault" is defined by database events. If the database doesn't capture all faults, period labels may be noisy.
  • S3 sampling: We sample 10-20 trips per period, not all trips. Anomalous trips may be missed.
  • Single site: Results are from E0269 only. Generalization to other sites/OEMs unknown.
  • Door faults dominate: E0269's faults are primarily door-related. Vibration may be more predictive for mechanical (non-door) faults, but we lack sufficient non-door fault data.

Required Data Volume for Production

  • Minimum: Daily vibration feature extraction (5 trips/day) per lift
  • Baseline period: 2+ months of normal operation to establish per-lift baseline
  • Recommended: 50+ lifts with 6+ months of continuous vibration + fault data for robust modeling
