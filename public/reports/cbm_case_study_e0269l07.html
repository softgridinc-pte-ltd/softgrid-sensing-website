  • Mar 26-27: Rapid escalation — 52 and 62 errors per day

Root Cause

The E2 error code, combined with the door reversal spike (3132 reversals in April, rate of 572.2‰ vs fleet avg ~45‰), points clearly to a door operator malfunction. The work order diagnosis confirmed this: "Car Door Alignment" issue requiring physical adjustment.

The door was likely misaligned, causing repeated close failures (E2), forced reversals, and safety edge activations (146 in April vs 24 in March). The lift continued operating but with severely degraded door performance for nearly 3 months before intervention.

9. Methodology Notes

Data Sources:

  • RMS Database: MySQL (localhost:3306/rms) — error codes, faults, work orders, door health signals, maintenance outcomes. Full history where available.
  • S3 Accelerometer: s3://lbb-simtech-raw/E0269L07A/ — 3-axis at ~100Hz per trip. 867 days of continuous data (2023-09-14 to 2026-03-12).

Signal Processing:

  • Gravity removal: z_net = (z - 1.0) × 9.81 m/s²
  • Velocity: cumulative trapezoidal integration of z_net
  • Jerk: time derivative of acceleration (np.gradient)
  • Lateral vibration: √(x² + y²) after median bias removal
  • ISO 18738 grading: 4th-order Butterworth high-pass (1 Hz) on z-axis, peak-to-peak in mg
  • PSD: Welch's method (256-point segments)

CBM Recommendations: Simulated based on actual data thresholds. Trigger logic: 3× baseline error rate → Warning; 10× → Critical; return to baseline → Resolved.
