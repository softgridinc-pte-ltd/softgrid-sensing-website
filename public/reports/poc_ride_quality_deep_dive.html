5. E0269L22 "Hidden Problem" Deep-Dive

Why this matters: E0269L22 has only 2 error codes in the entire 2.8-year history — by traditional metrics, it's one of the healthiest lifts in the fleet. Yet its ride quality is Grade D (36.1 mg), which is 2× worse than E0269L07 (23.4 mg) — the lift with the most database errors. This is exactly the kind of hidden degradation that accelerometer-based monitoring is designed to catch.

Anomaly Localization

Phase analysis:

  • Start phase peak acceleration: 0.7005 m/s²
  • Cruise phase peak acceleration: 0.8263 m/s²
  • Stop phase peak acceleration: 0.7365 m/s²

The highest vibration occurs during the Cruise (guide rail/motor) phase. This suggests potential issues with guide rail alignment, roller wear, or motor/gearbox vibration during travel.

What This Means for Maintenance

Traditional error-based monitoring would never flag L22 for inspection. Yet passengers experience a noticeably rougher ride every trip. Recommended actions:

  1. Inspect guide rails, rollers, and motor mounts — the vibration pattern points to this area
  2. Schedule a ride quality re-test after any maintenance to verify improvement
  3. Establish ride quality baselines for all 57 lifts to catch similar hidden issues

7. Frequency Domain Analysis

Power Spectral Density (PSD) reveals which frequency bands carry the most vibration energy. Different frequency ranges indicate different mechanical sources:

  • 0–2 Hz: Normal ride motion (acceleration/deceleration)
  • 2–10 Hz: Guide rail or roller issues
  • 10–30 Hz: Motor or gearbox vibration
  • 30+ Hz: Brake or sheave issues

8. Methodology Notes

For technical audience — this section describes the analysis methods used.

Data Acquisition

  • Raw 3-axis accelerometer data at ~100 Hz from S3 bucket (s3://lbb-simtech-raw/)
  • 5 trips sampled per lift from a recent date with verified movement (z-axis p-p > 5 mg)
  • Idle/stationary recordings automatically filtered out

Signal Processing

  • Bias removal: x-axis static bias (~0.012g) removed using median subtraction; z-axis gravity (1.0g) removed
  • Velocity: Cumulative trapezoidal integration of vertical acceleration (m/s²)
  • Displacement: Cumulative trapezoidal integration of velocity (m/s)
  • Jerk: First derivative of acceleration via np.gradient()
  • Lateral vibration: Magnitude of debiased x and y axes: √(x² + y²)
  • ISO 18738 grading: High-pass filtered (2nd order Butterworth, 1 Hz cutoff) vertical vibration, peak-to-peak in mg
  • PSD: Welch's method with 256-sample segments

Limitations

  • Velocity and displacement are estimated from single-integration of acceleration — drift accumulates over long trips
  • Sampling is limited to 5 trips per lift from a single day — seasonal/temporal variation not captured
  • Sensor mounting position and orientation assumed consistent across lifts (not verified)
  • ISO 18738 grading is based on filtered peak-to-peak, which may differ from formal testing methodology
